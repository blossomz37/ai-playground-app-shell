Chapter 1: The Arrival

The wind carried the scent of wet earth and pine, and somewhere beyond the trees, a river moved, patient and eternal.

Elara tightened the straps of her pack and stepped off the road, following the faint path her father had sketched in the margin of an old map. He had called this place a threshold, a seam between what is known and what remembers.

She had come seeking answers, though she was not sure she was ready for the questions that might find her instead. The village behind her was smoke and stone now, a story she hoped never to revisit.

Ahead, the trees parted just enough to reveal a valley cradled in morning mist. And there, half-buried in vines and time, stood the ruins. She exhaled. The journey had begun.
