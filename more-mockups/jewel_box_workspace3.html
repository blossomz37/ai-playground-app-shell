# Chapter 1: The Ash Falls

The sky above Eldoria had bled from its twilight purple into the bruised, heavy gray of an impending storm. [[Elara Voss]] tightened the frayed wool scarf around her neck, the chill biting through the threadbare coat she'd inherited from her brother. It wasn't just cold; it was the damp, metallic cold that preceded the Ash.

She paused near the entrance of the [[Sunken Market]], leaning against a rusted iron lamppost that hadn't held a flame in a decade. Below her, the market was a labyrinth of canvas tents and mud, lit only by the sputtering amber glow of chem-lanterns. The merchants were packing up early, their voices hushed, eyes repeatedly darting toward the western peaks.

**"They say it's moving faster this time,"** a voice muttered from the shadows behind her.

Elara didn't flinch. She recognized the rasp of Silas's breathing long before he spoke. She turned slowly, her hand instinctively grazing the hilt of the curved dagger concealed beneath her coat. *"Faster than what, Silas? The stories?"*

> "Faster than the warnings. The Vanguard fell two days ago. We're next."

A single flake of white drifted down, catching the ambient light before settling onto the sleeve of her coat. It didn't melt. She brushed it off, leaving a smear of chalky gray across the dark fabric. The Ash had arrived, and with it, the silent walkers.

Elara looked back at the city she had spent her life trying to escape. Suddenly, the urge to run was replaced by a strange, cold anchor in her chest. She drew the dagger, the [[Aetherite]] blade humming softly as it tasted the corrupted air.
